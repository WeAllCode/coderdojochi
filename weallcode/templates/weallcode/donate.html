Are there other ways to give?

Yes, here are a couple:

Benevity Workplace Giving Program

We All Code is pleased to be a participant in the Benevity Workplace Giving Program. When your employer becomes a member of the Benevity community, you are able to make donations to We All Code via payroll deduction. Many employers offer a matching contribution as well, increasing the amount that We All Code receives from your donation.

Benevity is available to employers in any country, and works with your companyâ€™s payroll department for ease of use from set up to payments.

Check with your employer to see if they participate in Benevity, or visit the Benevity site to find out more if your employer is not yet part of the Benevity program.

Amazon Smile

We All Code is a recipient for Amazon Smile. When you shop at Amazon, you can nominate for 0.5% of the purchase price of your eligible AmazonSmile purchases to be donated to We All Code.

Do you accept donations from companies/corporations?

Yes. We also have a corporate sponsorship program. Get in touch if you'd like to know more.

Can I choose a specific feature or project to support?

No: we don't allow donors to directly sponsor specific features.

However, we may be able to connect you to a Django developer who you can hire to work on a specific feature. Please get in touch if this sounds like something you'd like to pursue.

Is my donation tax deductible?

We All Code is a non-profit corporation; we have tax-exempt status under IRC 501(c)(3). This means that donations are tax-deductible for US citizens; donors outside of the United States should consult a tax advisor.

How can I donate if Stripe won't process my payment?

Please get in touch with us, and we'll figure something out that works for you.

Benefits of an Associate Board Membership

  • Friend-raising and networking experience
  • Leadership development experience and mentoring with the Board of Directors
  • Mentoring opportunities
  • Name recognition on Board invitations (when appropriate)
  • Recognition in the organization's Annual Report
  • Opportunities to enjoy special privileges such as behind-the-scenes tours and previews of new programs and initiatives

Membership Requirements

  • A passion for building future leaders and a commitment to developing students of courage, confidence and character.
  • Personally donate $250 (give) and work to bring in additional $500 (get) through personal/professional networks throughout the year.
  • Be an enthusiastic advocate for the mission of We All Code, especially among Chicago's young professional community.
  • Attend at least 75% of scheduled meetings, though attendance at all meetings is strongly encouraged.
  • Join an Associate Board committee.
  • Support fundraising events. This would include at least two of the following:
    • Secure monetary or in-kind donations.
    • Purchase at least one ticket and attend the event(s)
    • Make a personal financial donation towards the event if unable to attend
    • Purchase raffle tickets, silent auction items, etc. (if applicable)
  • Engage personal and professional networks and/or companies to support the organization at-large and Associate Board-specific events.
  • Commit to building students of courage, confidence and character, who make the world a better place and be passionate about developing future leaders.

The Associate Board currently consists of approximately 15 members. We aspire to grow while keeping membership limited. Our target members are professionals, entrepreneurs, and civic leaders in the first 5-10 years of their careers. Membership on the Associate Board is open to anyone who is passionate about We All Code's mission and interested in supporting the work we do.

Fundraising Goal

Each year, the Associate Board works with its liaison(s) from the Board of Directors to establish a fundraising goal for the fall event in addition to the Associate Board give/get requirements.

Associate Board Committees

Outreach - The Associate Board Outreach Committee works to increase the reach of We All Code, build a network/pipeline, and ensure that multiple dimensions of We All Code are represented.

Fundraising - The Associate Board Fundraising Committee uses creativity and connections, with a We All Code focus, to lead the annual fall event. This Committee focuses on leading the full Associate Board through the process of securing sponsorships, selling tickets, securing silent auction items, and creating an event that is engaging and mission-oriented.

Volunteer - The Associate Board Volunteer Committee works to create opportunities that engage the Associate Board members with the mission of the organization in a hands-on, meaningful way that also creates social interaction with each other.
